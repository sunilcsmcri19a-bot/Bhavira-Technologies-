Converting EPS (Thermocol) Waste into High-Performance Polymer Composite Electrodes

The Problem

In Victoria, expanded polystyrene (EPS), commonly known as thermocol, is widely used in construction and packaging applications by companies such as Visy Industries and Amcor, leading to significant environmental challenges. In 2018–19, an estimated 15,100 tonnes of EPS were consumed in Victoria, contributing to Australia's annual consumption of around 71,000 tonnes.

Although EPS producers and recyclers like Polyfoam Australia, Foamex, UNIPOD, StyroCycle (Expanded Polystyrene Australia), and Styrofoam Industries collect and process some clean EPS waste, the majority still ends up in landfill or as litter because routine kerbside collection is limited, contamination is common, and only a small fraction is actually reprocessed into new products.

Industry Challenge: Industries such as wastewater treatment (e.g., Veolia, SUEZ), metal recovery (e.g., Sims Metal Management), and environmental monitoring agencies require high‑performance electrodes for electrochemical processes. Current solutions—including metal electrodes, carbon electrodes, and screen‑printed electrodes—are expensive, hard to scale, and often cannot integrate chemical or surface modifications effectively, limiting industrial adoption and operational efficiency.

Our Solution

Bhavira Technologies converts EPS (thermocol) waste into advanced polymer composite electrodes (PCEs), creating a circular economy solution that addresses both industrial and environmental challenges. Our electrodes are scalable, cost-effective, and high-performing, enabling wastewater treatment, metal recovery, and environmental monitoring industries to operate efficiently.

Waste-to-Value Processing

We convert landfill-derived EPS waste into functional polymer composite electrodes using scalable and sustainable processing techniques with thermocol-derived carbon and graphite powder.

High-Performance Electrodes

Unlike conventional electrodes, PCEs integrate chemical and physical surface treatment during fabrication, enhancing performance while reducing costs.

Scalable & Industry-Ready

The technology is compatible with existing electrochemical systems, enabling rapid translation to industry for wastewater treatment and metal recovery applications.

Technical Validation


Bhavira Technologies has developed initial polymer composite electrodes (PCEs) using EPS waste and conducted comprehensive electrochemical characterization:

Electrochemical Testing

Cyclic voltammetry (CV), electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS), and differential pulse voltammetry (DPV) to evaluate conductivity, stability, and electrode performance

Durability Studies

Degradation studies under operational conditions to assess long-term stability and performance retention

Surface Characterization

SEM and AFM analysis confirming successful fabrication, surface morphology, and roughness optimization
